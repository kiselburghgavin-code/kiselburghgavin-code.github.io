Sonic Generations I love Sonic generations. It's my childhood and a great game. I still play it to this day. I guess if you want to get technical, it's Sonic x Shadow generations today. But I mainly love the Sonic generations part of the remaster. It goes through every main line Sonic game up to Sonic colors, which at the time, was the newest game in the series. The gameplay was fluent and it was a great platformer. It transitions from 2D sections with classic sonic with gameplay that resembles the original trilogy and Sonic CD, while the 3D sections follow modern Sonic with gameplay that resembles Sonic games today. I love the modern Sonic levels, being able to boost through with Sonic speed, destroying every enemy in your path! Don't even get me started on the bosses and rivals though. They were perfect. It was absolutely perfect. My favorite boss in the game is the Egg Dragoon. Second place has to go to Shadow though. Don't worry, I'll get you the link so you can buy it! Store page!
Yakuza 0 I love brutal combat. I love hand to hand combat. I love good story. Yakuza 0 has all three. Yakuza 0 is the prequel to the original Yakuza in 2005 or kiwami, which is the remaster of the original. It tells the back story of the Dragon of Dojima, Kazuma Kiryu along with the back story of the Mad Dog of Shimano, Goro Majima and how the two legends eventually cross paths at the end. While the story is good, the other half of the appeal is the combat. Most of the combat is hand to hand, with three... six... eight different fighting styles, but you can take a melee weapon or a gun into combat. I say eight styles because Kiryu has brawler, rush, and beast styles with his unlockable legendary Dragon of Dojima style. Majima has thug, slugger, and breaker styles with his unlockable legendary Mad Dog of Shimano style. Long story short for combat, Styles do different things and combos. Do different things and combos to build heat gauge. Use heat gauge to perform heat actions to deliver a devastating attack. Repeat until enemy is defeated. Extra steps include use weapons and items. That's it. But it does it really well. Nowadays, you're forced to buy the directors cut since they took the original off sale. Store page!
Shadow of war Like previously mentioned, I like Shadow of war. Shadow of war takes place in middle earth, specifically Lord of the rings as an open world story game. You play as Talion, a soldier that died after seeing his wife die in front of him and then resurrected from death by Celebrimbor, a... spirit, let's say. That's mainly Shadow of Mordor though, the prequel to this game (which is also really good!) Shadow of war, continues Talion's journey as possessing a ring of power, allows him to dominate enemy minds and resurrect the dead later in the story. Wielding these powers, he builds an army of orcs to battle Sauron sieging the different regions of Mordor. He also has extra powers that are combat orientated. For example, using a spiritual hammer, he can stun the enemy and freeze them. Following up with that, he can deliver twenty quick slashes with his sword, killing them. Speaking of combat, this game does it well. No hand to hand combat, but it replaces the disappointment of that with happiness with the brutal finishers. Blah, blah, blah, combat is good and brutal along with fluent, but what it really did that was revolutionary, was the nemesis system. With different events and enemy captains, the captains will remember you. I've seen gameplay of where an enemy captain came back to life 4 TIMES in a nemesis storyline before finally dying. I love the nemesis system. Unfortunately, they patented it, making it unavailable for any other game to use. That's the only thing I don't like about it. Store page!
Scott Pilgrim vs the world: the game Now hear me out. I love Scott Pilgrim. I love video games. Why not have both? This game is a side scroller beat em up where you play as Scott, Ramona, Stephen, and Kim! Scott and Ramona is self explanatory, but Stephen and Kim are from Scott's band, sex bob-omb. This game originally came out as an arcade game to celebrate the movie, but they released a complete edition for the tenth anniversary which had three more characters, Wallace, Knives, and a secret unlock that I'm not going to spoil. Wallace is Scott's gay roommate and Knives is a assassin high school girl that has a complicated history with Scott. I love the levels from this game. Who can't like a good beat em up? At the end of each level, an evil ex from the comics appear as a boss fight. That's where the game shines. I love the boss fights. My favorite is Nega Scott, which is basically, in the comics, him learning from his mistakes. I love the good beat em up genre and in this one, I get to play out my favorite comic series. Unfortunately, it doesn't have too good of a rating, but I still like it. Store page!
GTA San Andreas Arguably the best GTA game, San Andreas (SA) is my favorite game out of the series. At least, until GTA 6 comes out. You play as Carl Johnson (CJ) after getting called home because of his mom's passing. After getting called home, his story has betrayal, action, and juicy, JUICY plot twists. What I'm saying is that this game's story is the most iconic and just overall best story in the series. It's very immersive, being able to interact with NPCs around you with the d-pad. The combat is good and simple, based guns and cover for the most part. The cars are nice too, being able to mod them for performance and presentation. The game is an expansive open world, adding to the immersion and appealing to my interests. Got to love a good open world game. GTA SA is exactly that. Store page!
